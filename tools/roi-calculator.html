What is ROI (Return on Investment)

Return on Investment (ROI) is a key financial metric used to evaluate the profitability of an investment. It measures the return generated relative to the investment’s cost. In simple terms, ROI tells you how much profit or loss you’ve made on an investment as a percentage of the original amount invested.
For example, if you invested $1,000 and earned $1,500 in return, your ROI would be 50%. This metric is crucial for businesses and individuals alike to assess the success of their investments over time.

Types of ROI

There are various types of ROI calculations depending on the investment type and purpose:

  • Standard ROI — Used for general investments to calculate overall returns.
  • Annualized ROI — Accounts for the duration of the investment to give a yearly return percentage.
  • Social ROI (SROI) — Used for social enterprises to measure returns on social and environmental benefits.
  • Marketing ROI — Focuses on returns generated from marketing campaigns relative to their cost.

Factors Affecting ROI

Several factors influence the ROI of an investment:

  • Investment Duration — The longer the investment is held, the more time it has to grow, which can result in higher returns. The ROI calculator can help you assess both short-term and long-term investments.
  • Investment Type — Different types of investments (stocks, bonds, real estate, etc.) have varying levels of risk and return potential. The ROI formula remains the same, but the returns vary based on the type of asset.
  • Costs and Fees — TSome investments come with hidden fees or management costs that can reduce the overall return. It’s essential to consider these costs when calculating your ROI.
  • Market Conditions — Economic factors such as inflation, interest rates, and market volatility can impact the returns on investments. Understanding these conditions can help you predict the potential ROI.

How to Calculate ROI

To calculate ROI manually, use the following formula:

(Amount Returned - Amount Invested) Amount Invested × 100

For Annualized ROI, the formula is:

(( Amount Returned Amount Invested ) 1 Duration (in years) - 1 ) × 100
